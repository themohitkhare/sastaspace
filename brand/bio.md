## Bio — long (≤180 words, for About page / conference bios)

> Mohit Khare is a software engineer based in Bengaluru, with several years of experience building and optimising large data systems. He spends his day job on the unglamorous end of software — making slow things fast, expensive things cheap, fragile things dependable.
>
> His real fun lives at **sastaspace.com**: a personal lab where he builds the things he wants to build and shares them with the world. Every project gets its own subdomain, shares one database and one design language, and stays live as long as it's useful. Some of the work is polished. Some is a prototype he hasn't touched in months. All of it is out in the open.
>
> The name is a promise. *Sasta* is the Hindi word for *affordable* — and at this lab, that cheapness is the feature, not the flaw. Sasta projects can keep shipping, keep changing, and keep being shared without needing to justify themselves. Expensive ones usually don't ship at all.

## About-page copy (drop-in for the site)

### One-paragraph hero
> Hi — I'm Mohit. This is my lab. I build the things I want to exist, then put them somewhere you can use them. Some of it works. Some of it's half-built. Everything here is sasta by design: cheap to make, cheap to run, open to share.

### Two-column "what the lab is / isn't"

**What it is**
- A workshop on the open internet.
- A place to ship things quickly and keep them live.
- A single design language across every experiment.
- Open by default — open URL, open source, open to feedback.

**What it isn't**
- A startup. No team, no pitch deck, no fundraise.
- A portfolio. Portfolios edit for wins; labs show the whole bench.
- A resume. Jobs and metrics live elsewhere.
- A roadmap. Projects get built when they're interesting and shipped when they're ready.

## Project card microcopy template

Each project card on sastaspace.com follows this shape. Keep it brutally short. No metrics — a *status chip* and a one-line pitch.

```
<subdomain>.sastaspace.com         <-- mono, sasta orange
<project name>                     <-- sans, 500
<one-line pitch — ≤12 words>       <-- sans, 400, dust
[status] [tech] [tech]             <-- status chip + mono tags
                                       status ∈ {live, wip, paused, archived, open source}
```

Examples:
- **notes.sastaspace.com** · Notes · Plain-text notes. Keyboard-first. Live-synced.
- **feed.sastaspace.com** · Feed · A reader for the RSS corner of the web.
- **pipes.sastaspace.com** · Pipes · Visual builder for small data jobs.
